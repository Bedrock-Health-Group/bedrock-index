== Purpose

The purpose of this HIPAA Compliance Policy is to ensure that Bedrock Health Group (BHG) maintains the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Protected Health Information (PHI) in accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA).

== Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who handle PHI, including but not limited to electronic, paper, and oral formats.

== Responsibilities

* **Compliance Officer:** Oversees HIPAA adherence, conducts audits, and manages training programs.  
* **Employees & Contractors:** Must follow all procedures to protect PHI and report any breaches immediately.  
* **IT Department:** Implements technical safeguards, monitors systems, and manages access controls.

== Policy Requirements

=== Administrative Safeguards

* Maintain workforce training on HIPAA policies and procedures annually.  
* Conduct regular risk assessments to identify and mitigate potential PHI threats.  
* Ensure access to PHI is limited to authorized personnel only.

=== Physical Safeguards

* Secure physical access to facilities where PHI is stored.  
* Implement policies for workstation use and device disposal.  
* Maintain logging of physical access to PHI storage areas.

=== Technical Safeguards

* Use encryption for PHI stored or transmitted electronically.  
* Implement unique user IDs and strong authentication for system access.  
* Enable automatic log-off and audit controls on all systems handling PHI.  

== Breach Notification

All HIPAA breaches must be reported immediately to the Compliance Officer. A documented investigation will occur, and affected parties will be notified according to HIPAA breach notification rules.

== Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, and may also result in civil or criminal penalties under HIPAA.
